Interactive Experience Research Project

Aisha Chiam - s4098357

Website: Sculpting Harmony

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was the immersive landing page that immediately drew me in with the bold, rounded display typography. At the same time, the background music started playing and I felt like it really complimented the visuals.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

In the first two minutes, I scrolled down from the landing page to explore the introduction, clicked into the first chapter, and kept going over interactive models and graphics to view more information throughout the first two minutes. I moved between sections, read the text while listening to the background music, and occasionally stopped to look at the images before continuing.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time exploring all three chapters, paying attention to the models and pictures, reading the texts, and watching the architect ‘Frank Gehry’ talk about the design process. The mix of visuals, written content, and spoken insights kept me engaged throughout.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action I did was probably scrolling through the content and different chapters, alternating with clicks to expand the visuals or view interactive models.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal seems to be to share the creative journey of Frank Gehry’s Walt Disney Concert Hall, highlighting his design process through sketches, models, interviews, and immersive visuals.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

It creates a narrative flow that encourages learning and exploration through the use of archival material, engaging visuals, background music, and a chapter-based structure.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It seems to be made for both short visits and in-depth curiosity, allowing users to revisit particular chapters or take their time diving deeper into the overall story.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The variety of multimedia components, highlight sections, and straightforward chapter navigation allows users to go at their own pace and go back to the material as needed.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

It merges elements of physical exhibitions, 3D digital modeling, and recorded performances, similar to how hybrid museum experiences combine in-person and online content.

10) What does this references communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

It recommends interacting with the experience as a component of a more comprehensive educational process. Taking time to listen, observe, and connect the digital materials with their physical and cultural context.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

It encourages a sense of reflection, curiosity, and appreciation for creative expression, similar to attending a concert or visiting a museum.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

To be honest, I didn’t find any part of the interaction frustrating. Everything felt smooth, intuitive, and easy to navigate, which made the experience enjoyable from start to finish.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part was how the music, images, and interactive elements came together to provide a deep, multi-sensory understanding of Frank Gehry's artistic process.
